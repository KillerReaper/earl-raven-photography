I am someone who learned to see by standing quietly behind the lens. For nearly a decade, I have chased light and shadow — not to control them, but to understand the way they linger, the way they reveal meaning when left undisturbed.

I study Film and Moving Image to deepen that understanding, to explore how moments breathe when they are allowed to unfold naturally. Photography and motion have become inseparable to me — two expressions of the same instinct to observe, to frame, to remember.

What began as quiet experimentation has developed into a disciplined practice. Through the lens and in post-production, I refine atmosphere, composition, and tone, shaping images that feel intentional without feeling constructed.

Much of my work is informed by the natural world — landscapes, wildlife, and environments that demand patience rather than direction. Alongside this, experimental imagery continues to influence the way I approach form and mood.

More recently, my work has expanded to include documenting people and shared moments — where timing, trust, and sensitivity become just as essential as technical precision. Each subject offers a different challenge, and each one reshapes the way I see the next.

But above all, this work is about presence. About waiting long enough for something real to surface. About recognizing the moment when observation becomes intention.

These images are not conclusions. They are evidence of a way of seeing.
