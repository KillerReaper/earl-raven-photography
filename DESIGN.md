## Design Purpose
This document defines the **design philosophy, structure, and behavior** of the Earl Raven Photography website.

Its purpose is to ensure the website remains visually consistent, intentional, and aligned with the artistic identity of **Earl Raven (Sasha DeBenedictis)**, regardless of future updates or changes.

This document focuses on **intent and behavior**, not technical implementation.

---

## Core Design Philosophy
- The website should feel like a **gallery or editorial publication**
- The interface should **never compete with the photography**
- Simplicity, spacing, and restraint communicate professionalism
- The website should feel **timeless**, not trendy

The photography is always the primary focus.

---

## Color & Visual Rules
- Website interface uses **black, white, and gray only**
- No accent colors, gradients, or decorative effects
- All color on the site comes from the photographs themselves
- Backgrounds may be light or dark depending on page mood, but always neutral

White space is intentional and used to give images room to breathe.

---

## Typography Direction
- Editorial-style typography
- Serif fonts for headings to convey elegance and artistic tone
- Clean, readable fonts for body text
- Thin to regular font weights
- Generous spacing between text elements

Text should feel calm and confident, never loud or aggressive.

---

## Navigation Philosophy
- Navigation should be minimal and unobtrusive
- Page titles are clear and descriptive
- No unnecessary dropdowns or visual clutter
- Navigation exists to guide, not to dominate the page

---

## Page Behavior & Intent

### Home
**Purpose:** First impression

- Establish tone and mood immediately
- Use a strong hero image or minimal headline
- Very limited text
- Direct visitors toward the Master Gallery

The Home page should feel like entering a gallery space.

---

### Master Gallery
**Purpose:** Showcase best work

- Curated selection of the strongest photographs
- Represents the highest level of quality
- Images are given prominence and space
- Browsing should feel calm and intentional

This is the primary portfolio page for clients.

---

### Archives
**Purpose:** Show depth, growth, and experimentation

- Houses extended bodies of work
- Same visual language as the Master Gallery
- Content may be organized into subsections

Possible subsection approaches include:

**By time or phase**
- Beginning Work
- Experimental Work
- Personal Projects

**By subject or theme**
- Nature
- Flowers
- Animals
- Cars
- Food
- Other focused subjects

Subsections are flexible and may evolve over time.

---

### Information
**Purpose:** Define identity and experience

- Focused entirely on Earl Raven (Sasha DeBenedictis)
- Artist statement and creative mindset
- Background and experience
- Notable projects, recognition, or achievements
- May include portrait imagery

This page builds trust and understanding without selling.

---

### Blog
**Purpose:** Credibility and storytelling

- Client reviews and testimonials
- Short write-ups about shoots or projects
- Occasional behind-the-scenes or reflective posts

This page does not require frequent updates. Quality over quantity.

---

### Contact
**Purpose:** Direct communication

- Dedicated space for inquiries and bookings
- Simple, professional input fields
- Allows visitors to submit their contact information and message
- Submissions notify Earl Raven directly

The Contact page should feel clear, calm, and respectful.

---

## Interaction Guidelines
- Animations, if used, must be subtle and purposeful
- Transitions should feel smooth and natural
- No distracting motion or aggressive effects
- Interactions should support focus, not draw attention to themselves

---

## Content Rules
- Minimal text throughout the site
- No filler content
- Every element must have a reason to exist
- Images are never overcrowded

---

## Long-Term Intent
This website is designed to:
- Grow with the artist
- Remain visually consistent over time
- Avoid redesign pressure due to trends
- Serve as a stable, professional portfolio

All future changes should respect the principles outlined in this document.
